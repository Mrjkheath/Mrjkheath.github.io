Calculating Molarity generally requires 3 steps:

1. Calculate the moles of solute in the solution

2. Calculate the number of liters in the solution

3. The moles of solute is then divided by the liters of solution

Often times problems contain grams of a solute.
If this is the case, the grams must be converted to moles. This is simply converted by dividing
the grams given in the problem, by the molar mass of the solute itself.

You'll often encounter problems where you're
given milliliters instead of liters for solution
measurements. However, all calculations for
molarity involve liters.
So you must convert the millileters into liters.
You must divide the milliliters by 1000.
