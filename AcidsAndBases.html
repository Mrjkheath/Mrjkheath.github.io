Acid and Base Chemistry

Converting between pH, pOH, [H+], and [OH-]

pH is the measure of hydronium ion within a solution.
pH is often measured in the form of litmus paper with
a scale ranging from 0-14. The lower the number, the
higher the concentration of the hydronum ion in the solution.
this quality is known as acidity.

pOH is the opposite of pH. pOH is the measure of the
hydroxide ion within a solution. pOH has a scale similar to pH
ranging from 0-14. However, the lower the number, the higher
the concentration of the hydroxide ion. This quality is known
as basicity.

Often times when talking about pH and pOH, there
will also be associated calculations to determine the
hydronium [H+] and hydroxide ion concentration [OH-]
respectively.
